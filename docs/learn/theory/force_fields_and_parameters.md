# Theory: Force Fields and Molecular Interactions

## What are Force Fields?

In molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, a **force field** is used to calculate the interactions (and therefore the forces) between all the different atoms and molecules in your system.

A force field consists of two main components:

1.  **Mathematical Functions (Functional Forms):** These are the equations that describe different types of physical interactions. Examples include:
    *   **Harmonic Oscillator:** Models bonded atoms as springs.
    *   **Lennard-Jones Potential:** Describes non-bonded attraction and repulsion.
2.  **Parameters:** These are specific values plugged into the equations for different atom types. They define properties like bond stiffness, ideal angles, and partial charges.

### Breakdown of Interactions
Force fields typically divide energy and forces into:
*   **Bonded Interactions:**
    *   **Bonds:** Stretching between directly connected atoms.
    *   **Angles:** Bending between three connected atoms.
    *   **Dihedrals (Torsions):** Twisting around a central bond (four atoms).
*   **Non-Bonded Interactions:**
    *   **Van der Waals Forces:** Modeled with the Lennard-Jones potential.
    *   **Electrostatics:** Modeled using Coulomb's law for partial charges.

## LAMMPS and OpenFF Interactions

When LAMMPS uses parameters generated by OpenFF (like the "Sage" force field), it maps them onto standard Class I functional forms. LAMMPS doesn't have a specific "OpenFF" setting; instead, it uses its built-in mathematical styles.

### 1. Non-Bonded Interactions (Pair Style)
LAMMPS typically uses styles like `pair_style lj/cut/coul/long`.
*   **Van der Waals (Steric):** Standard Lennard-Jones 12-6 potential using epsilon (well depth) and sigma (distance) from OpenFF.
*   **Electrostatics:** Coulomb's Law using partial charges, often paired with a long-range solver like `kspace_style pppm`.

### 2. Bonded Interactions
OpenFF parameters are plugged into these LAMMPS styles:
*   **Bonds:** `bond_style harmonic` (spring constant $K$ and ideal length $r_0$).
*   **Angles:** `angle_style harmonic` (force constant and ideal angle).
*   **Dihedrals (Torsions):** `dihedral_style fourier` or `multi/harmonic`/`opls` (periodic cosine series).
*   **Impropers:** `improper_style cvff` or `harmonic` (maintaining planarity).

### 3. Special Bonds (1-4 Interactions)
OpenFF requires specific scaling for atoms separated by three bonds. This is controlled via:
`special_bonds lj 0.0 0.0 0.5 coul 0.0 0.0 0.8333`
This scales the Lennard-Jones force by 50% and the Coulomb force by 83.33% for 1-4 interactions.
